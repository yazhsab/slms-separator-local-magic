# Quantum Scope Statement

This manuscript fits *Quantum* as a quantum information and classical simulation paper. It introduces a separator-local simulation parameter for structured tensor-network/circuit families with stabilizer and non-stabilizer components. The main theorem gives a randomized additive simulation algorithm for locally marginalizable resource-decomposition networks, with cost controlled by stabilizer-compressed effective width and an explicit message-local quasiprobability burden.

The work is positioned between tensor-network simulation and magic-resource simulation. It does not claim an unconditional quantum advantage. Instead, it provides a theorem and algorithm clarifying when structured compositional quantum language circuits are classically easy, and why dense treewidth or global non-Clifford counts can be too coarse when stabilizer compression and local marginalization are available.

The QNLP material is a restricted specialization and benchmark motivation, not an application claim. The paper contributes a simulation theorem, certified parameter definitions, separation examples between parameter regimes, and reproducible synthetic accounting scripts.
